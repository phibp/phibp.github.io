website revamp!

i spent tonight, up until around 10:30pm, working on the website revamp. changes include an oversized top bar and the addition of this blog.

but thats not interesting- lets hear about the BUGS! the ISSUES! what did i MESS UP?

well, for starters, this very blog had a glaring issue, in that the post box thing didnt work! though, i had just forgotten to add the white background in the stylesheet for the blog. boringggggg!

the obvious thing to have issues would be our star attraction: the top bar!

to have it carry over through each page was quite the chore - because it actually doesnt!

for each little change i made to it, i would actually copy it to every. single. page. though, i might remove the bar from some more obscure pages, not for that reason, but just because it looks nicer sometimes.

i also had absolutely no way to know if the bar worked properly, due to the stylesheet that controls it being located at "/" which is a website directory, rather than one on my computer. luckily, "/" is where the home page is located, so on that page i can simply tell it to look for it in its own folder.

as for every other page on the entire site - you just had to hope! not to mention, you had no idea what the stylesheet could do to anything other than the top bar.

you see, the stylesheet applies the same style to all instances of the specified function.

sound like mumbo jumbo to ya? well, no worries!

think of the stylesheet as a cop, and the kinds of functions used to create the top bar as black men.

basically, the cop is racist, and will arrest all of the black men that it finds, even if they are law abiding citizens (not being used to create the top bar)

what the stylesheet actually DOES, is contain code that adds the background image and makes the links work properly, but through a seprate .css file rather than having to add that code to each page individually.

the main problem was due to what it does with the home/shop/blog links. those links are contained in a LIST function, and the stylesheet tries to apply that same organisation to ALL LISTS. see why its a problem?

luckily, whatever the stylesheet does is always overruled by simply specifying otherwise for that specific list.

not so luckily, i had no idea that anything had even happened to those lists, because the stylesheet is inaccessible when the website is located on your computer. i wasnt aware of those problems until AFTER i uploaded it to github, and saw my precious mixtape tracklist get scrambled like eggs.

to fix it, i had to figure out what to overrule, and apply it to all lists, which took a moment. at one point i even had copied an extremely long line of code to each ENTRY of each list, which did actually work, but i realised an easier way to do it about 5 minutes later. lifes like that, huh?
